## How translators should use these abstracts and PDFs

1. **For papers with PDF**: open the full PDF and read the relevant section
   (Methods, Results, Discussion) before translating a passage that references
   this paper's content. The full text is the authoritative source for
   terminology and phrasing.
2. **For papers without PDF (abstract only)**: use the abstract as a terminology
   anchor. If deeper context is needed, access the full text via institutional
   library or contact the corresponding author.
3. **Terminology anchor**: before translating a Chinese passage that mentions a
   specific method or clinical concept, find the closest paper in this index.
4. **Phrasing templates**: study how the abstract reports sensitivity, specificity,
   AUC, cohort sizes, training details. Mirror these patterns.
5. **Register calibration**: the abstracts demonstrate the formal academic English
   register expected by Q1/Q2 SCI journals.
6. **Citation verification**: if your source text cites a paper that appears in
   this knowledge base, you can verify the citation details against the table above.
